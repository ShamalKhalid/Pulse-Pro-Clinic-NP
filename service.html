Things We Don’t Do

Certain medical conditions require immediate in-person care. If you experience any of the following, please seek emergency medical attention at your nearest hospital.

Requests or Refills for Controlled Substances

Medications requiring special handling and oversight.

Severe & Acute Conditions

  • Acute/severe abdominal pain
  • Life or limb-threatening conditions
  • Acute chest pain
  • Severe shortness of breath
  • Extreme lethargy
  • Severe headache
  • New difficulty breathing

Neurological Issues

  • Stroke symptoms (slurred speech, weakness)
  • Loss of consciousness
  • Sudden vision decline
  • Acute paralysis or progressive motor loss

Infant-Related Concerns

  • Fever in babies under 3 months
  • Fever for more than 3 days (ages 3-12 months)

Diabetes & Blood Sugar Issues

Severe & persistent high blood sugar (>20 mmol/L) with symptoms like extreme thirst, fatigue, or dizziness.

Severe Stomach Issues

Severe abdominal pain with nausea/vomiting.
